Film

Media Sketches

Spring 2020 The following "media sketches" are 1-2 minute short films, exploring a variety of topics, themes, and styles. I wrote, shot, and edited a new video each week, using equiptment ranging from my IPhone camera to a Canon C100 Mark II. (note: videos are embedded from Vimeo & may take a moment to load)

Video Surveillance

This project topic is inspired by Xu Bing's 2017 Documentary/Mystery Film Dragonfly Eyes, as well as excerpts from Alfred Hitchcock's Rear Window, and work done by the "Surveillance Camera Players."

  • Prompt: Create a surveillance video that plays with the issues of seeing as knowing, privacy, control, paranoia and technology.

Film Diary

This project topic explores the documentation of everyday happenings to capture parts of the filmmaker's life. Inspired by the works of Jonas Mekas, such as Lost Lost Lost, and George Kuchar's Weather Diaries.

  • Prompt: Create an autobiographical vignette without using dialogue or narration.

Dream Sequence

This media sketch displays the whimsicle, vibrant, and strange realm of dreams.

  • Prompt: Shoot and edit a dream sequence.

Portrait of Two Places

Using previously shot footage from my camera role, this film connects and jumps between the landscapes of California and Germany. The video prompt is loosely inspired by the 1927 silent film Berlin, Symphony of a City.

  • Prompt: Shoot a landscape or portrait of a place that tells us something about how you feel about that location.

Abstraction

Explores the showcasing of colors, shapes, motion, and sound in an abstract filming and editing style. Inspired by the works of Oskar Fischinger, Mary Ellen Bute, Enrique Pineda Barnet, and Stan Brakhage.

  • Prompt: Create a short abstract video.

Motion Graphics

Spring 2019A short animated film made using After Effects. I completed this as a final project in the course "Foundations of 2D Design."

Super 8 Film

Fall 2015This film black and white silent film was created during my apprenticeship with Pittsburgh Filmmakers, in which I worked on a team of four students to write, shoot, produce, and edit. We shot this film on a Super 8 camera, and developed and processed the film ourselves.

The Red Wheelbarrow

Spring 2014This short film was inspired by The Red Wheelbarrow, a poem by American modernist poet William Carlos Williams.
